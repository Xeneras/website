Chimera

Home Page |  About the author | Sources| Definition

The Chimera was, according to Greek mythology, a monstrous fire-breathing hybrid creature of Lycia in Asia Minpr, composed of the parts of more than one animal. It is usually depicted as a lion, with the heat of goat arising from its back, and a tail that might end with a snake's end, and was one of the offspring of Typhon and Echidna and a sibling of such monsters as Cerberus and the Lernaean hydra.
